    Common Cyber Attacks

    • Phishing

      Tricks users into revealing sensitive info via fake emails or websites.

    • Malware

      Software designed to damage or gain unauthorized access to systems.

    • Ransomware

      Encrypts your data and demands payment to restore access.

    • SQL Injection

      Exploits input fields to execute malicious database queries.

    • DDoS

      Overwhelms systems with traffic, causing downtime or crashes.

    • XSS

      Injects scripts into websites to hijack sessions or steal data.

    Social Engineering

    Learn how attackers manipulate people to gain access to private information.

    Read More

    Types of Social Engineering

    • Pretexting

      Attackers create a fake scenario or "pretext" to get you to reveal information.

    • Baiting

      Uses a false promise to entice a victim, like a "free movie download" that contains malware.

    • Quid Pro Quo

      Offers a service in exchange for information, e.g., a fake IT support call requesting your password to "fix a problem."

    Cloud Security

    Understand the risks and best practices for securing your data and applications in the cloud.

    Read More

    Key Cloud Security Measures

    • Access Control

      Implement the principle of least privilege, ensuring users only have access to what they absolutely need.

    • Data Encryption

      Encrypt data both in transit (while it's being sent) and at rest (while it's stored) to prevent unauthorized access.

    • Regular Audits

      Conduct security audits and penetration testing to identify vulnerabilities in your cloud environment.

    Protecting Your IoT Devices

    • Change Default Passwords

      Always change the factory-set passwords on new devices like routers, cameras, and smart home hubs.

    • Network Segmentation

      Isolate IoT devices on a separate network (e.g., a guest network) to prevent attackers from using them to access your main computers.

    • Disable Unnecessary Features

      Turn off any features or services on your devices that you don't use to reduce the attack surface.

    Safeguarding Your Personal Information

    • Review Privacy Settings

      Check and adjust the privacy settings on all your social media and app accounts regularly.

    • Use a VPN

      A Virtual Private Network (VPN) encrypts your internet traffic, hiding your online activity and location.

    • Be Mindful of Permissions

      Think twice before granting apps access to your contacts, photos, or location.
